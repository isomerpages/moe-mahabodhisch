<h4><strong>Vision</strong></h4>
<p>To develop inspired learners with exemplary character through the mastery
of problem-solving skills and E21CC competencies.</p>
<h4>&nbsp;<strong>Mission</strong></h4>
<p>To nurture confident problem-solvers and self-regulated learners with
an interest and love for Mathematics.</p>
<h4><strong>Key Approaches to Teaching and Learning of Mathematics</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Developing Critical, Adaptive and Inventive Thinking (CAIT) in Our Pupils</strong>
</p>
<p>The Mathematics Department intentionally develops pupils’ Critical, Adaptive
and Inventive Thinking (CAIT) through the learning of Mathematics. Pupils
are provided with explicit opportunities to apply CAIT through short open-ended
tasks and Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL) activities. These approaches encourage
pupils to analyse problems, consider multiple strategies, and justify their
thinking. As a result, pupils develop a stronger ability to tackle non-routine
and complex problems, demonstrate greater flexibility in applying different
strategies, and gain confidence in exploring alternative solutions.</p>
<p><strong>Promoting Metacognition in Our Students</strong>
</p>
<p>The Mathematics Department adopts a school-wide approach to promote metacognition
in all students. Teachers consistently use the <strong>UPS√ framework</strong> to
guide pupils in planning, monitoring, and evaluating their problem-solving
processes. Through the explicit teaching of metacognitive strategies, pupils
are supported in becoming more self-regulated learners who are aware of
how they think and learn. This structured approach helps pupils develop
more effective problem-solving strategies, become more aware of their learning
gaps, and gain greater confidence in tackling non-routine mathematical
problems. More details about the UPS√ framework are provided below.</p>
<p><strong>Developing Confident and Independent Problem Solvers</strong>
</p>
<p>The Mathematics Department &nbsp;places a strong emphasis on developing
pupils into confident and independent problem solvers. To support this,
the Mathematics Department has adopted the <strong>UPS✓ Framework</strong>,
a structured approach that guides students through the process of solving
mathematical word problems.</p>
<p>The UPS✓ Framework is adapted from the well-known problem-solving process
introduced by mathematician George Pólya. It helps pupils approach problems
in a clear and systematic way by encouraging them to think about the problem
before performing calculations.</p>
<p><strong>UPS✓ stands for:</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>U – Understand</strong>
</p>
<p>Pupils read the problem carefully and identify key information. They consider
what the problem is about and what is being asked.</p>
<p><strong>P – Plan</strong>
</p>
<p>Pupils decide how they will approach the problem. They may represent the
problem using models, diagrams, or mathematical expressions.</p>
<p><strong>S – Solve</strong>
</p>
<p>Pupils carry out their chosen strategy to find the solution.</p>
<p><strong>✓ – Check</strong>
</p>
<p>Pupils review their answers to ensure they make sense and that the problem
has been solved correctly.</p>
<p>Using the UPS✓ Framework, pupils develop structured mathematical thinking
as they learn to understand the problem, plan appropriate strategies, solve
systematically, and reflect on whether their solutions are reasonable.
Teachers support this process by using question prompts that guide pupils’
thinking and encourage them to explain their reasoning.</p>
<p>The UPS✓ Framework is implemented progressively across all levels from
Primary 1 to Primary 6, allowing pupils to develop stronger problem-solving
habits over time. As pupils become more familiar with the framework, they
grow in confidence and develop the ability to tackle increasingly complex
problems with independence.</p>
<p>By nurturing these habits of thinking, our school aims to equip pupils
with the skills, perseverance and confidence to solve problems in both
mathematics and in real-world situations.</p>
