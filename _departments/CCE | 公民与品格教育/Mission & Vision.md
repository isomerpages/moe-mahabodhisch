<p><strong><u>Character and Citizenship at the Heart of Learning</u></strong>
</p>
<p>At Maha Bodhi School, Character and Citizenship Education (CCE) lies at
the heart of our efforts to nurture students who uphold strong values,
care for others and contribute meaningfully to society. Anchored in the
school vision of <strong>“Inspired Learners of Exemplary Character,”</strong> the
CCE Department strives to nurture students of integrity who lead with purpose
and empathy while serving others with heart.</p>
<p>Aligned with the school mission of nurturing <strong>confident lifelong learners and compassionate contributors with a deep connection to Chinese culture</strong>,
our CCE programmes develop civic-minded and compassionate citizens grounded
in strong values and cultural identity.</p>
<p>Through purposeful learning experiences and authentic opportunities for
action, students internalise values, strengthen relationships and grow
into responsible individuals who contribute positively to society.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong><u>Alignment to CCE 2021 Curriculum and 21st Century Competencies</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Guided by our department’s vision and mission, the school’s CCE programmes
are aligned with the <strong>CCE 2021 Curriculum</strong> and the development
of <strong>21st Century Competencies</strong>, equipping students with the
dispositions and skills needed to navigate an increasingly complex world.</p>
<p></p>

<p>Organised around three key ideas of <strong>Identity, Relationships and Choices</strong>,
our curriculum guides students in understanding themselves, building positive
relationships and making responsible decisions.</p>
<p>At the heart of CCE are the shared values of <strong>Respect, Responsibility, Resilience, Integrity, Care and Harmony</strong>,
which guide students to act with integrity, show care for others and contribute
positively to their communities.</p>
<p>Through explicit teaching, authentic learning experiences and everyday
teachable moments, students develop <strong>self-awareness</strong> and <strong>self-management</strong>,
strengthen their <strong>social awareness</strong> and <strong>relationship management</strong>,
and learn to practise <strong>responsible decision-making</strong>. Through
these experiences, they grow as confident persons, active contributors
and concerned citizens.</p>
<h4><strong><u>Focus Area 1: Character Building</u></strong></h4>
<p><strong>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Positive Education through the HEARTS Framework</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Character education focuses on nurturing students’ values, well-being
and sense of purpose. Through intentional structures and meaningful learning
experiences, students develop the dispositions, social-emotional competencies
and resilience needed to thrive in school and life.</p>
<p>At Maha Bodhi School, Positive Education is enacted through the <strong>HEARTS Framework</strong>,
which shapes how students experience well-being, belonging and character
development in their daily school lives.</p>
<p>Guided by the <strong>PERMAH framework</strong>, HEARTS focuses on:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Positive Health and Engagement</strong> – Creating safe and supportive
environments that promote students’ well-being.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Positive Action</strong> – Empowering students to take initiative
and contribute meaningfully through purposeful projects and service.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Positive Relationships</strong> – Building a caring school culture
where students support and uplift one another.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Positive Thinking</strong> – Cultivating a growth mindset, mindfulness
and resilience to navigate challenges with confidence.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>SAP Ethos</strong> – Deepening appreciation of Chinese culture
while strengthening understanding of Singapore’s multicultural identity.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through HEARTS, Maha Bodhi School fosters a caring and empowering environment
where every student can flourish as a confident and compassionate learner.</p>

<p><strong>2. <u>Education and Career Guidance (ECG) @ Maha Bodhi School</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Education and Career Guidance (ECG) complements the CCE curriculum by
helping students develop self-awareness, discover their strengths and explore
possibilities as they grow in purpose and character.</p>
<p>At Maha Bodhi School, ECG is guided by the framework of <strong>Discovering Purpose, Exploring Opportunities and Staying Relevant</strong>.
Students deepen their understanding of their interests, values and strengths
while appreciating how different occupations contribute to society.</p>
<p>The ECG journey unfolds progressively across the primary years:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Primary 1–2:</strong> Exploring creative industries through design
activities and reader’s theatre.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Primary 3:</strong> Learning about healthcare professions through
role-play and first-aid awareness.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Primary 4:</strong> Deepening self-awareness through reflection
and dialogue with professionals.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Primary 5–6:</strong> Exploring education pathways through <strong>MySkillsFuture,</strong> secondary
school talks and alumni sharing.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through these experiences, students connect their strengths and interests
with their aspirations while developing adaptability, resilience and a
stronger sense of purpose.</p>
<p><strong>3. <u>Nurturing an Attitude of Gratitude</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Cultivating gratitude is an important aspect of character development
at Maha Bodhi School. Students learn to appreciate the contributions of
those around them and recognise the value of kindness and service.</p>
<p>Through school programmes and everyday interactions, students express
appreciation to both teaching and non-teaching staff who support them.
A meaningful tradition is the <strong>Teachers’ Day Tea Offering</strong>,
where students honour their teachers through a ceremony rooted in Chinese
cultural values.</p>
<p>These experiences nurture humility, respect and gratitude, shaping students
who appreciate others and are committed to caring for and serving the community.</p>
<h4><strong><u>Focus Area 2: Citizenship &amp; Civic Responsibility</u></strong></h4>
<p><strong>4. <u>Values-in-Action (VIA) @ Maha Bodhi School</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Citizenship education provides meaningful opportunities for students to
understand their role in the community and nation while empowering them
to contribute positively to society.</p>
<p>At Maha Bodhi School, VIA is structured as a <strong>progressive developmental journey across the primary years</strong>,
aligned with MOE developmental milestones. This developmentally staged
journey strengthens a caring and enabling school culture where every student
learns to contribute meaningfully.</p>
<p><strong>Lower Primary (P1–P2): Building Self and Immediate Care:</strong>
</p>
<p>Students develop self-awareness, responsibility and care within their
immediate circles through projects such as <strong><em>Project I Can and Project I Care</em></strong>.
Structured and scaffolded experiences help students cultivate empathy and
positive habits as they learn to care for family members, peers and the
school community.</p>
<p><strong>Middle Primary (P3–P4): Expanding to Community Contribution</strong>:</p>
<p>Through initiatives such as <strong><em>Project We Can</em></strong> and <strong><em>Project We Care</em></strong>,
students extend their care beyond themselves to the wider community. With
teacher guidance, they learn to work collaboratively to conceptualise and
carry out projects that support meaningful causes, including caring for
the environment and appreciating members of the community.</p>
<p><strong>Upper Primary (P5–P6): Leading for Social Impact</strong>:</p>
<p>Students take on greater ownership of their learning through projects
such as <strong>Integrated Project Work (IPW) on Sustainability and Project Pay It Forward</strong>.
These student-driven initiatives encourage students to address community
needs, strengthening their sense of agency, leadership and responsibility
in contributing to society.</p>
<p>Through this progressive VIA journey, students move from caring for themselves
and their immediate circles to contributing actively to the community,
growing into socially aware individuals who seek to make a positive difference.</p>
<p><strong>5. <u>Commemoration of NE Core Events</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Citizenship education is strengthened through the commemoration of National
Education (NE) Commemorative Days, including Total Defence Day, International
Friendship Day, Racial Harmony Day and National Day.</p>
<p>These platforms deepen students’ understanding of Singapore’s history,
multicultural identity and shared responsibility in safeguarding the nation’s
future while strengthening their sense of belonging as citizens.</p>
<p><strong>6. <u>Developing Student Voice and Civic Awareness</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Beyond national awareness, Maha Bodhi School encourages students to engage
thoughtfully with contemporary issues and develop the confidence to express
their perspectives.</p>
<p>Through the <strong>Hear Me Out</strong> programme, students participate
in guided discussions on current issues. <strong>National Education (NE) Ambassadors play an active role in facilitating these conversations</strong>,
leading peers to explore different viewpoints and reflect on issues that
impact society. Dialogue and reflection help students listen to diverse
perspectives, articulate their ideas and consider the impact of their choices
on others.</p>
<p>Through these experiences, students develop confidence and civic awareness
to engage constructively with societal issues and contribute as active
citizens.</p>
<p><strong><u>Nurturing Character and Citizenship</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Through the CCE Department’s intentional efforts to develop both character
and citizenship dispositions, students grow as values-driven and socially
responsible individuals. These experiences empower them to care for others,
engage thoughtfully with society and contribute meaningfully to the community
and nation.</p>
